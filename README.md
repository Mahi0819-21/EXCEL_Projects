### 1. 🛒 Shopping List Problem Solving
**File:** `Shopping_List_Problem_Solving.xlsx`

**What it does:**  
Compares prices of 15 everyday items (Ball Point Pen, Calculator, Notebook, Glue, Markers, Stapler, etc.) across 3 stores — **Walmart, Dollar Trap, and Office Repo** — to find the cheapest shopping option for a given budget.

**Key features:**
- Multi-store price comparison table for 15 items
- Calculates total cost per store automatically
- Built a **Susan Chart** — grouped bar chart comparing item prices across all 3 stores
- Built a **Dollar Chart** — bar chart showing total spend per store
- Result: Walmart = $82.79 ✅ (cheapest), Dollar Trap = $87.54, Office Repo = $103.29
- Used SUM formulas, data organisation, and conditional formatting to highlight the lowest total

**Skills demonstrated:** Data comparison, charting, SUM formulas, decision-making with data

### 2. 💰 Loan Payment Analysis & Comparison
**File:** `Loan_Payment_Analysis.xlsx`

**What it does:**  
Analyses and compares 4 loan options (Loan A, B, C, D) all at $20,000 principal over 12 months at varying interest rates (6%, 7%, 8%, 9%) to determine the most cost-effective borrowing option.

**Key features:**
- Calculated **Interest Paid**, **Total Loan Paid**, and **Monthly Payment** for each loan
- Used financial formulas to compute payments automatically
- Built a bar chart: **"Monthly Payment for $10K Loan"** visualising payment differences across interest rates
- Clear insight: Higher interest rate = significantly higher monthly payment (9% = $1,816.67 vs 6% = $1,766.67)

**Skills demonstrated:** Financial formula modelling, PMT-style calculations, data visualisation, comparative analysis

### 3. 📝 The Grade Book
**File:** `The_Grade_Book.xlsx`

**What it does:**  
A comprehensive student performance tracker for a class of 18 students across multiple test categories including **Safety Test, Company Policy, and Financial Skills Test**.

**Key features:**
- Tracked scores for 18 students across 4 assessment columns
- Calculated percentage scores per student per test
- Auto-calculated **Max, Min, and Average** scores for each test category
- Used **conditional formatting** with colour indicators (green/yellow/red dots) to flag performance levels
- Built a **TRUE/FALSE flag column** to identify students needing follow-up
- Created **3 bar charts**: Safety Test, Company Policy, and Financial Skills Test performance per student
- Class averages: Safety Test 89%, Company Policy 85%, Financial Skills 82%

**Skills demonstrated:** IF logic, percentage calculations, conditional formatting, MAX/MIN/AVERAGE functions, multi-chart dashboarding

---

### 4. 🚗 Car Inventory Management
**File:** `Car_Inventory.xlsx`

**What it does:**  
A detailed vehicle inventory database tracking 20+ cars across multiple attributes — used for real-world style fleet or dealership data management.

**Key features:**
- Structured dataset with 14 columns: Car ID, Make, Model, Manufacture Year, Age, Miles, Miles/Year, Color, Driver, Warranty Miles, Covered status, New Car ID
- Used **MID formula** (`=MID(A4,3,2)`) to extract model year from Car ID automatically
- Calculated **Miles per Year** for each vehicle to assess usage rate
- Applied **conditional formatting** to highlight high-mileage vehicles (Miles/Year column)
- Built a **scatter plot** — Miles vs Age of the car — showing usage trends over vehicle lifetime
- Tracks warranty coverage (Y / Not covered) per vehicle

**Skills demonstrated:** MID/text formulas, calculated columns, data structuring, scatter plot visualisation, large dataset management

### 5. 📈 Sales Report Dashboard
**File:** `Sales_Report.xlsx`

**What it does:**  
A multi-sheet sales analysis report for a pool supply business tracking transactions across multiple months, products, salespeople, and locations (NM, CA, AZ, CO).

**Key features:**
- **Sheet 1 (Raw Data):** Full transaction log with columns — Month, Transaction Number, Product Code, Product Description, Store Cost, Sale Price, Profit, Commission (10% for items under $50, 20% for items over $50), Salesperson name, Location
- Applied **SUMIF** formula for conditional commission calculation
- Used **text to columns**, **sort**, and **filter** tools for data cleaning and organisation
- Built **Pivot Table** on Sheet 2 summarising total sales by salesperson
- Created a **Pie Chart** showing sales contribution by salesperson: Barns $6,003.50, Smith $5,661.10, Johnson $3,035.30, Hernandez $2,410.70 — Grand Total: $17,110.60
- Tracks profitability per product with real margin analysis

**Skills demonstrated:** Pivot Tables, Pie Charts, SUMIF, IF formulas, data cleaning, multi-sheet workbooks, profit & commission modelling

### 6. 💼 The Career Decision
**File:** `The_Career_Decision.xlsx`

**What it does:**  
A weighted decision-making model built in Excel to objectively compare 5 career options using multiple scored criteria — helping make data-driven life decisions.

**Key features:**
- Evaluated 5 career paths: McDonald's, Doctor, NFL, **Engineer**, Truck Driver
- Scored each career across 5 weighted criteria: **Pay (weight: 3), Job Market (weight: 5), Enjoyment (weight: 4), My Talent (weight: 3), Schooling (weight: 1)**
- Calculated weighted total score for each career automatically
- Used conditional formatting to highlight the **winning option: Engineer = 65** (highest score)
- Result: Engineer ranked #1, followed by Truck Driver (53), NFL (52), Doctor (51), McDonald's (49)

**Skills demonstrated:** Weighted scoring models, decision matrix design, multiplication formulas, conditional formatting, data-driven decision making
